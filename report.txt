Day 11: 22 April

PROGRESS UPDATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Data Engineering & Preprocessing

Temporal Aggregation: Consolidated country-year pairs into a single-row-per-country "averaged" snapshot to simplify data for the start

Transformation Strategy: Moved from StandardScaler to PowerTransformer (Yeo-Johnson) because the data was quite skewed

Implemented Log-Scaling for GDP and Energy Use per capita to capture relative differences

Feature Pruning: Removed highly correlated features to prevent multicollinearity from biasing the clusters. also played around with what the clusters look like if we remove any economic indicators

2. Modeling & Iteration

K-Means & Hierarchical Clustering: Played around with archetypes that form using kmeans, also did autoencoder and then kmeans. also tested hierarchical clustering. found
that the clusters do change but they are pretty consistent at a level. Problem is I have nothing to bench mark against.

Finding: Removing economic indicators shifted the clusters quite significantly

Dimensionality Reduction (PCA vs. Autoencoders):Used PCA for initial visualization but found "one big cloud."Implemented a PyTorch Autoencoder to learn a "Latent Space" (3D representation).

The Overfitting Challenge: Acknowledged the risk of overfitting due to the small sample size. 

Addressed this by implementing fixed seeds and "trust scores" to check for cluster stability across runs.

3. Current Archetypes Emerging

"Golden Standard" (DNK, NZL, SWE): Consistently isolated as leaders, appearing as outliers in DBSCAN.

"Innovators" (CHL, MEX, COL): Interestingly grouped with Nordic countries when economic data is removed—showing they have "advanced" portfolios despite lower GDP.

"Industry Heavy" (CHN, IND, POL, AUS): Grouped by heavy industry dependencies and specific implementation-focused policies.

"Laggards" (RUS, SAU, ISR): Grouped by a sparse policy framework regardless of wealth.

4. Current Challenges & Next Steps

Cluster Stability: Investigating why Autoencoder + K-Means yields different results per run.  Also tested this by including some made up countries that I expected to land in a certain place... often correct.

Solution: Implementing consistency checks and centroid analysis.

DBSCAN Limitations: Noted that density-based clustering is struggling with the current density of the latent space (resulting in one giant cluster).

Future Scope: Considering the inclusion of NDCs (Nationally Determined Contributions) and "Rate of Change" metrics to see who is accelerating their climate action vs. who is stagnant.

Current Experimenting: testing how they change with the inclusion of different variables.

Future: think I should look into which variables are currently dominating


5. Thoughts:

Is it more valuable to cluster countries as they are (Economy + Policy) or by their intent (Policy only)?

Would it make sense to keep K-Means on raw data as a form of control to account for the possibility of ae overfitting?

Variable Choice: I've currently been including a random selection of policies that I chose thinking they cover a fair deal of policy groups but of course this could be a 
bad group I have no way of knowing.
